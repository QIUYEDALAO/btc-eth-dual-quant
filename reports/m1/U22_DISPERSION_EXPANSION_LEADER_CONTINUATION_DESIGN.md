When cross-sectional return dispersion broadens and one active member develops
positive peer-relative leadership across multiple completed observations, the
move may reflect segmented attention, slow capital rotation and gradual price
discovery rather than a fully incorporated one-bar jump. Relative leadership
may therefore persist beginning no earlier than the next eligible open.

This design reads no public data or outcome and selects no timeframe, history,
common-return estimator, dispersion estimator, expansion rule, coherence rule,
threshold, horizon, cost Gate or strategy rule. The exact statistic core must
pass a synthetic-only complexity check before the later protocol is frozen.
OOS remains sealed.
